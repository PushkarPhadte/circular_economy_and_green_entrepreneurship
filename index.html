What is the Circular Economy and Green Entrepreneurship?

The Circular Economy and Green Entrepreneurship together form the foundation of a sustainable future. While the circular economy aims to minimize waste, reuse resources, and regenerate natural systems, green entrepreneurship brings those principles to life through innovations that combine economic success with environmental responsibility.

Why Do We Need It?

Our world faces increasing waste production, resource depletion, and environmental degradation. Circular models and green innovation are key to building smarter cities, sustainable businesses, and resilient communities. They help combat the climate crisis, create green jobs, and secure a better life for future generations.

  • Reduce pollution and waste going into oceans and landfills
  • Promote clean energy and green manufacturing
  • Support environmental justice and rural livelihoods
  • Encourage responsible production and consumption

The Consequences of Inaction

🔍

Air and industrial pollution threaten global health and biodiversity.

🔍

Plastic waste is filling oceans faster than it can be recycled.

🔍

Deforestation causes habitat loss and accelerates climate change.

🔍

Soil pollution causes a wide range of negative impacts on the environment, human health, and ecosystems.

Core Principles

Design Out Waste

Imagine products, buildings, and services that create little or no waste at all. It's about using fewer resources and increasing the lifespan of what we make.

Keep Resources in Use

Promote sharing, reusing, repairing, refurbishing, and recycling—so resources continuously cycle through our economy and environment.

Regenerate Nature

Support practices that restore natural systems—such as composting, renewable energy, and biodiversity projects—so our planet can thrive.
