Circular Economy & Sustainability

Creating value by reusing resources – the future of sustainable progress.

What is a Circular Economy?

A circular economy is an economic model focused on minimizing waste and maximizing resource reuse. Products are designed to be repaired, refurbished, and recycled instead of discarded.

Why Does It Matter?

  • Reduces reliance on raw materials and conserves resources
  • Minimizes environmental pollution and landfill waste
  • Promotes innovation in product design and business models
  • Supports sustainable communities and green job growth

Why is our consumption pattern a problem?

Unsustainable consumption patterns are a major driver of global environmental degradation and are central to many of the world's most pressing challenges. These patterns contribute significantly to climate change, with household consumption responsible for up to 72% of global greenhouse gas emissions and consumption accounting for up to 80% of total resource use in affluent societies. The environmental impact extends beyond production, as the use and disposal of goods, such as automobiles causing pollution and urban sprawl, and food packaging ending up in landfills, create substantial degradation

Evolution of Circular Economy

1960s-70s

Conceptual Origins

The circular economy concept emerged from systems thinking, industrial ecology, and ecological economics, challenging the traditional linear "take-make-dispose" model.

1987

Brundtland Report

The UN's "Our Common Future" report defined sustainable development, laying groundwork for circular economy principles and global environmental policy.

2010s

Global Formalization

Ellen MacArthur Foundation popularized circular economy, creating frameworks adopted by businesses and governments worldwide for systemic change.

2020

India's Commitment

Goa became India's first state with a comprehensive Resource Efficiency & Circular Economy Strategy, setting national precedent.

2022-Present

Rapid Expansion

EPR regulations, digital governance platforms, and massive investments drive India's circular economy transformation with 157 lakh tonnes recycled.

2029-2050

Future Vision

Global circular economy market projected at $798B by 2029, with India's $2 trillion economy creating 10 million green jobs by 2050.

Real-World Circular Economy in India

  • ReCircle: An innovative Mumbai-based startup, ReCircle collects, sorts, and recycles waste through a digital platform, supporting over 2,500 waste workers and enabling responsible consumer and brand participation in recycling.
  • Indore & Surat Municipal Corporations: These cities have transformed their waste management. Indore’s large-scale composting and plastic recycling operations have set national benchmarks, while Surat’s wastewater treatment plant supplies industrial-grade water, easing demand on groundwater.
  • PET Plastic Recycling: India recycles nearly 90% of its PET plastic waste, well ahead of global averages—thanks to a strong informal sector and regulatory support. These efforts reduce landfill loads and provide income for thousands of workers.
  • Fashion Brands like Doodlage & Sirohi: Indian fashion brands remake textile waste into new apparel and lifestyle goods, creating circular systems in one of the country’s largest polluting industries.

India's Waste Management Transformation

The Challenge: India generates approximately 62 million tons of waste annually, with over 75% remaining unprocessed. Plastic waste alone accounts for 12% of total waste generated.

The Progress: Only 20-25% of total waste was recycled a few years ago, but recent initiatives are rapidly changing this scenario. India's plastic waste recycling capacity is set to grow from 10.9 million tons (2024) to 25.4 million tons by 2033—a CAGR of 9.37%!

Government Initiatives Driving Change

  • Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR): Market-based regulations covering e-waste, vehicles, plastic packaging, batteries, tyres, and used oil. Recyclers earn additional profits from EPR certificate sales.
  • Digital Governance: National Plastic Waste Reporting Portal launched on World Environment Day 2025 mandates online reporting for transparency.
  • Plastic Packaging Targets: Recycling targets increase from 50% (2025) to 80% (2028) for rigid/flexible plastics, and 30% to 60% for other categories.
  • Mission LiFE & Eco-Mark Rules: Boost demand for environmentally friendly products and sustainable lifestyles across India.
